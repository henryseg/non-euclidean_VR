<abstract> flow( v ) → {Position}

Description

Flow the current position. v is the pull back at the origin by the position of the direction in which we flow The time by which we flow is the norm of v.

The procedure goes as follows. Let e = (e1, e2, e3) be the reference frame in the tangent space at the origin. Assume that the current position is (g,m) The vector v = (v1, v2, v3) is given in the observer frame, that is v = d_og m u, where u = u1 . e1 + u2 . e2 + u3 . e3.

  • We first pull back the data at the origin by the inverse of g.
  • We compute the position (g',m') obtained from the initial position (id, id) by flowing in the direction w = m u. This position send the frame m e to d_o g' . m ' . m . e
  • We move everything back using g, so that the new observer frame is d_o (gg') . m' . m e.

Hence the new position (gg', m'm) is obtained by multiplying (g,m) and (g',m')
